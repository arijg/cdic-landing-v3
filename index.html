Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

SDS (Swiss Dental Solutions) implants are manufactured in Switzerland from medical-grade zirconia ceramic — entirely free of metal. Unlike titanium, zirconia does not release metal ions into surrounding tissue, does not corrode, and does not create galvanic reactions. SDS implants are developed under the SWISS BIOHEALTH Concept, a science-based biologic protocol emphasising systemic compatibility, natural healing, and long-term tissue harmony. Additionally, zirconia is tooth-white — meaning no dark metallic show-through at the gumline, even as gum tissue naturally recedes with time.
For standard All-on-4 cases with sufficient bone volume, your treatment covers everything from start to finish: consultation, CT scan, all necessary extractions, 4 SDS zirconia implants, ceramic abutments, PRF biologic healing protocol, laser-assisted surgery, all follow-up visits, and your final solid zirconia arch bridge. Cases needing extensive sinus floor elevation, complex bone reconstruction, or zygomatic implants are assessed individually. You receive a full written treatment plan at your consultation, before any commitment is made.
Ceramic zirconia implants are specifically the preferred choice for patients with metal sensitivities, titanium hypersensitivity, or autoimmune considerations. Because SDS zirconia is a biologically inert ceramic oxide — not a metal — it does not trigger metal-related immune responses or release ions into your system. Many of our patients choose us precisely because titanium was not appropriate for their health profile. We always conduct a thorough health history review at consultation to ensure ceramic implants are the right choice for your specific situation.
The All-on-4 technique places four implants strategically in the jawbone — two straight at the front and two angled toward the rear — to maximise use of available bone and provide immediate, stable support for a full-arch bridge. The angled rear implants avoid the need for bone grafting in most cases. Over 20+ years of clinical evidence confirms that four well-positioned implants provide sufficient support for a complete arch of teeth. Dr. Goldschlag uses 3D digital planning and guided surgery to ensure precision placement every time.

Our team is experienced in coordinating care for out-of-area patients and can structure treatment around travel schedules where possible. The boutique setting at 120 Central Park South means you receive personal, attentive care regardless of where you are coming from.
PRF (Platelet-Rich Fibrin) is a biologic technique that concentrates your own blood's growth factors and applies them directly to the surgical site. It significantly accelerates healing, reduces post-surgical inflammation, supports natural bone and soft tissue regeneration, and improves implant integration outcomes. We include PRF in every full-arch case because it aligns with our biologic philosophy — using your body's own resources to optimise healing — and because it directly improves the long-term success of your ceramic implants.
